DevGriot's Top 3 Movies: According to Him

...and the 3 Winners Are...

1. Claudine (1974)

I value this film for its raw, honest portrayal of navigating systemic financial hurdles. It’s more than just a story; it’s an exploration of how one maintains integrity and builds a foundation while trapped in a "welfare state" economy. It resonates with my focus on community resilience and the strategic maneuvering required to overcome institutional barriers.

2. The Book of Eli (2010)

Eli represents the ultimate disciplined strategist. In a world where information has become the most valuable currency, he survives through a combination of specialized knowledge and a singular, tactical mission. As someone who views information and legacy as power, I relate to his commitment to protecting a "blueprint" for the future at all costs.

3. Strictly Business (1991)

This movie perfectly captures the intersection of corporate strategy and cultural authenticity. It’s about the "hustle" in its most sophisticated form—learning to navigate high-level professional environments without losing your roots. It reflects my own journey of balancing professional growth with a grounded, community-focused mindset.
